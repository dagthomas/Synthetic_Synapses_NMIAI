### Key scoring insight: static cells don't matter at all!

Ocean and mountain cells have ground truth entropy = 0, so they contribute zero
to the entropy-weighted KL divergence. All our effort to predict them precisely
(floor optimization, static locking) has ZERO scoring impact.

**100% of the score comes from dynamic cells** — anything near settlements.

## Architecture Changes Made Today

### 1. Fixed 3×3 grid → 100% map coverage
- 9 viewports at (0,13,25) × (0,13,25) = all 1600 cells covered
- Uses 45 of 50 queries, leaves 5 for adaptive 2nd observations
- Previous greedy approach: only 81% coverage with gaps

### 2. Cross-seed pooling (GlobalTransitionMatrix)
- Pools terrain transitions across all 5 seeds
- Hidden parameters are shared → 5× more data per transition type
- Adaptive blend weight: n/(n+5) → 98% trust with 200+ observations
- Key to detecting this round's collapse: 270 settlement observations → 62% became empty

### 3. Parameter estimation from settlement stats
- Analyzes population, food, wealth, defense, faction structure
- Detects regime: winter harshness, trade activity, expansion rate
- Adjusts Dirichlet prior strength: 0.9 for divergent rounds, 3.0 for R1-like rounds
- Computes prior adjustment multipliers

### 4. Per-cell adaptive floor
- Dynamic cells (near settlements): floor = 0.005
- Static cells (far from everything): floor = 0.001
- Reduces KL cost from floor by ~50% on confident cells
- Note: doesn't affect score for ocean/mountain (entropy=0)

### 5. Per-cell prior strength
- Settlement cells: strength × 0.5 (trust observations more)
- Far cells: strength × 2.0 (trust prior more)
- Prevents one noisy observation from corrupting a confident prediction

### 6. Settlement stats for per-cell adjustment
- Thriving settlements (pop>2, food>0.5): boost survival probability
- Starving settlements (food<0.2): boost collapse probability
- Dead settlements: dramatically reduce settlement/port probability

### 7. Adaptive queries target 2nd observations
- With 100% coverage, 5 remaining queries re-observe most dynamic cells
- 2nd stochastic sample reduces variance more than covering new static cells

## What top teams probably do differently

The gap to #1 (85.5) is significant. They likely:
1. **Use all 50 queries for 2nd+ observations** — if their priors are good enough,
   they don't need 100% coverage and can get 2-5 observations per dynamic cell
2. **Have better initial priors** from more rounds of data
3. **Model spatial correlations** — if a settlement survived, its neighbors likely did too
4. **Use the actual simulation mechanics** from `mechanics.md` to build analytical models
   rather than purely empirical approaches
