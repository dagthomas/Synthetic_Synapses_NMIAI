# Q* Frontier — Intro Video Manuscript

## Overview
**Duration**: ~3 minutes
**Style**: Cinematic cyberpunk — dark, neon-lit, with holographic UI overlays
**Music**: Synthwave/darksynth, building tension → triumphant
**Voice**: Low, gravelly narrator — think "Blade Runner" briefing officer

---

## Scene 1: The Void
**Duration**: 15 seconds
**Visual**: Camera drifts through deep space. Stars streak past. A distant planet glows with an eerie cyan shimmer on its surface. The planet slowly rotates, revealing crystalline veins pulsing with light across its dark surface.

**Voice Line**:
> *"In the year 2187, humanity discovered Q-Star minerals — exotic crystalline substances found only on uncharted worlds at the edge of known space. Worth more than entire star systems... if you can predict where they'll grow."*

---

## Scene 2: Orbital Approach
**Duration**: 15 seconds
**Visual**: A sleek corporate scout ship descends toward the planet. Holographic HUD overlays appear — scanning grid lines, coordinate markers. The ship's viewport shows a 40×40 planetary grid materializing below. Neon scan beams sweep across the surface.

**Voice Line**:
> *"Each planet is governed by hidden physics — 26 variables that control how minerals grow, spread, compete, and decay. No two planets are alike. Every cycle, the surface transforms."*

---

## Scene 3: The Surface
**Duration**: 20 seconds
**Visual**: Close-up of the planetary surface. Camera sweeps across terrain types:
- **Neon cyan crystals** pulse and grow (Crystal Nodes)
- **Dark magenta refineries** hum with energy near void edges (Refineries)
- **Bright green xenoflora** shifts and sways, feeding nearby crystal growth
- **Obsidian ridges** tower in the background, impassable and eternal
- A **depleted vein** crumbles to dust as its minerals are exhausted
- Crystals suddenly spread to a neighboring cell — expansion in action

**Voice Line**:
> *"Crystal Nodes grow on the surface — fueled by nearby Xenoflora, shaped by planetary physics. They expand, they compete, they die. Refineries process minerals at the void's edge. And when conditions turn harsh... entire deposits collapse into depleted veins."*

---

## Scene 4: The Viewport Limitation
**Duration**: 15 seconds
**Visual**: Overhead view of the full 40×40 grid — but most of it is hidden in fog of war. A small 15×15 viewport window lights up in neon cyan, revealing a section of the planet. The window moves to another location — a different stochastic result appears. A counter in the corner ticks: "SCANS: 3/50".

**Voice Line**:
> *"But here's the catch — you can only see fragments. Each orbital scan reveals a 15-by-15 window of the surface. One stochastic snapshot. You have 50 scans per planet. Shared across five scan zones. Choose wisely."*

---

## Scene 5: The Hidden Parameters
**Duration**: 20 seconds
**Visual**: A holographic control panel materializes, showing 26 mysterious sliders — all locked and glowing red. Labels flash: "BASE_SURVIVAL", "EXPANSION_SCALE", "FACTION_KILL_PROB", "WINTER_SEVERITY". The sliders shift and morph, showing how different values produce wildly different outcomes. Split-screen: same starting planet, three different physics → collapse / moderate growth / explosive boom.

**Voice Line**:
> *"Twenty-six hidden parameters govern every planet. Survival rates. Expansion curves. Faction conflict intensity. Winter severity. Trade bonuses. You can't see them. You can't measure them directly. But every scan you take carries their fingerprint. Learn to read between the crystals."*

---

## Scene 6: The Prediction
**Duration**: 15 seconds
**Visual**: A corporation analyst sits at a holographic terminal. Their hands sweep across a 40×40 probability heatmap — cells glow with varying intensities of cyan, magenta, green. They drag confidence sliders. The prediction tensor takes shape: each cell shows six probability bars (Crystal, Refinery, Depleted, Xenoflora, Obsidian, Void). They hit "SUBMIT" — the tensor beams up to an orbital relay.

**Voice Line**:
> *"Your mission: predict the probability distribution of every cell on the planet after 50 growth cycles. Six classes. Forty by forty cells. 9,600 probabilities. Your prediction versus ground truth — scored by entropy-weighted divergence."*

---

## Scene 7: The Competition
**Duration**: 15 seconds
**Visual**: Multiple corporate logos appear on a holographic leaderboard — neon-lit rankings shifting in real-time. Score numbers tick up. A corporation jumps from #3 to #1. Split-screen showing three different teams' prediction heatmaps overlaid on the actual ground truth. The best prediction glows green; the worst pulses red.

**Voice Line**:
> *"You're not alone out here. Rival corporations are scanning the same planets. The better your model, the more accurate your predictions, the higher you climb. Every fraction of a point matters. Overconfidence kills — assign zero probability to anything, and a single ground truth hit destroys your score."*

---

## Production Notes

### Visual Style
- **Color palette**: Deep blacks (#0a0a1a), neon cyan (#00fff0), magenta (#ff00ff), gold (#ffd700), toxic green (#39ff14)
- **Typography**: JetBrains Mono, monospaced throughout
- **Effects**: Scanlines, holographic distortion, grid overlays, particle systems for crystal growth
- **UI overlays**: Glass panels with subtle cyan border glow, breathing animations

### Key Concepts to Communicate
1. **Hidden parameters** — the core mystery (26 unknowns per planet)
2. **Limited observations** — 50 scans, 15×15 viewport, stochastic results
3. **Probability prediction** — not binary classification, full distributions
4. **Scoring fairness** — entropy-weighted, only dynamic cells count, never assign zero
5. **Competition** — real-time leaderboard, multiple rounds/planets
6. **Regime diversity** — barren, temperate, volatile planets feel completely different
